The list of references is managed with \texttt{biblatex} (IEEE style) in the file \texttt{reference.bib} and printed automatically in the REFERENCE section. In accordance with the citation policy of this research, only peer-reviewed articles published in reputable Q1/Q2 journals are used as references. Below are a few examples illustrating the citation format.

C. J. L. Murray et al., ``Global burden of bacterial antimicrobial resistance in 2019: a systematic analysis,'' \textit{The Lancet}, vol. 399, no. 10325, pp. 629--655, 2022 \cite{murray2022global}.

A. J. Page et al., ``Roary: rapid large-scale prokaryote pan genome analysis,'' \textit{Bioinformatics}, vol. 31, no. 22, pp. 3691--3693, 2015 \cite{page2015roary}.

S. G. Earle et al., ``Identifying lineage effects when controlling for population structure improves power in bacterial association studies,'' \textit{Nature Microbiology}, vol. 1, no. 5, 2016 \cite{earle2016identifying}.

Publicly available software (e.g.\ scikit-learn, XGBoost) and databases (CARD, ResFinder) are referenced through their corresponding journal publications where available; datasets and code repositories are cited inline via their URLs rather than as bibliography entries.
